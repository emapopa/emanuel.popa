About Me

Emanuel Popa

Ux/ui designer with background in frontend

I moved to Stockholm, Sweden in 2015. At that time, I was working as a chef. Many of my friends were in the IT sector, which piqued my curiosity and led me to consider a career change. However, I wasn't sure which direction to take. I had a strong interest in design, so I began studying full-stack web development, thinking it would provide more opportunities. As I progressed with my studies, I realized that I wanted more control over the design aspect, and I found that programming didn't interest me as much as I initially thought. After obtaining my full-stack diploma, I decided to study UX design with the goal of gaining a better understanding of the creative process from idea to design. In the meantime, I started working as an assistant teacher at the same school for HTML & CSS and JavaScript courses, where I helped students grasp challenging concepts and corrected their projects for six months. My motto is to balance design with usability, ensuring that neither aspect is neglected. While I studied UX two of my schoolmates and I founded a student organization of which I am one of the presidents. My role involves overseeing the courses. I frequently lead lessons and workshops for first-year students, covering topics such as HTML & CSS, as well as UX & UI design, which I thoroughly enjoy. I'm someone who enjoys having a lot on my plate.

H&M Dropdown menu

A/B Testing

Our hypothesis is that adding a hamburger menu will make it easier for users to navigate the site and find the information they need, resulting in increased engagement and a better user experience.

Improve user experience: One of the primary goals of adding a hamburger menu to H&M's website would be to improve user experience. By making it easier for users to navigate the site and find the information they need, they will have a more positive experience and be more likely to return in the future. Increase Engagement: Another goal of the A/B test would be to increase engagement on the website. If users can more easily find what they are looking for, they may spend more time on the site and be more likely to make a purchase or take some other desired action. Measure Impact: The test would also aim to measure the impact of adding a hamburger menu to the website. By defining specific metrics to measure, such as click-through rate, bounce rate, time on site, and conversion rate, we can determine whether the changes had a positive, negative, or neutral impact. Optimize navigation: Finally, the test would aim to optimize the navigation on the H&M website. By testing different versions of the website, we can identify which navigation options work best for users and use that information to create an even better user experience. The purpose of the A/B test on HM's website is to identify the most effective menu design to increase user interaction and improve their experience. By testing two different versions of the menu, we can measure and compare user behavior and evaluate which design is most effective in achieving our goals. By using the A/B testing method, we can draw reliable conclusions about which design is most successful and use this knowledge to improve the user experience and increase engagement on HM's website.

H&M fast checkout

A/B Testing

If the product list is improved and the fas checkout function is implemented, it will lead to approx. 5-6 percent more purchases. The goal is for the new version to increase the number of purchases and to see how high the probability is that the new version will surpass the existing one.

In this step, the produced data is analysed. First of all, it is analyzed if there is a difference in the number of purchases between the versions. What is measured is the number of purchases and not the sum of the purchases because the aim is to see if more people will be inclined to complete a purchase if the process is faster and smoother. Then it is analyzed if there is any difference in how many visitors only surf around the page without adding anything to the shopping cart in both versions. This is analyzed because if there are more visitors who only browse and do not add anything to the cart in version A (existing page) than B (new page), it indicates that the supermarket feature may be a factor influencing the propensity to add items in the shopping cart. Of course, there may be other factors that influence, but since only one change is tested at a time, it is concluded that it is the supermarket function that affects. In order to conclude that the testing is statistically significant, the same test would be performed twice to ensure that the test results are not influenced by external factors and then a chi2 test would be performed to measure the significance of the data and ensure that the result is not due to chance . A chi2 test is done with the formula: X^2=∑(o-E)^2/E.

Implement boards in Slack

Businnes Proposal

My project idea is to integrate boards into Slack. For those who are not familiar, Slack is a messaging app designed specifically for businesses. It helps connect people to the information they need, and by doing so, transforms the way organisations communicate. With Slack, you can easily message anyone inside or outside your organisation, making it simple to collaborate remotely. You can even work in dedicated spaces called channels that bring together the right people and information. Nowadays, there are many online boards available, such as Miro and Figma. However, when we need to collaborate visually with other coworkers, we need to move outside the platform. That's where my idea comes in. I propose integrating a new feature called board groups. This will allow users to collaborate visually within Slack, without having to switch between different tools.

Here are a few reasons why people might use whiteboards in Slack:

  • Brainstorming

    Whiteboards can be used to brainstorm ideas with the team. Members can share their thoughts and visually map out ideas in real-time, which can help generate new ideas and get everyone on the same page.

  • Planning

    Teams can use whiteboards to plan out projects and tasks, assign responsibilities, and track progress. This can help keep everyone on track and ensure that projects are completed on time.

  • Problem-solving

    Problem-solving When facing a problem, whiteboards can be used to visually identify the issue and come up with potential solutions. Team members can collaborate and work through the problem together, which can lead to better results.

  • Presentations

    Whiteboards can be used to create and share presentations with the team. Members can collaborate on the presentation in real-time, add notes, and share feedback.

Chas website re-design

Requiremnt Gathering

The Purpose of the project is to get the user who visits the Chas Academy webpage apply to the courses that they offer, and to achieve that it is needed to gather all the requirements from the Stakeholders The customers  are the users, like people who want to start to study or people who already study but want to change course or career. Other customers are the Firms who can apply to business courses . The client is the Chas Academy organization. Other stakeholders is the Chas business management and the designer who is in charge for gathering all te accessibility requirements (WCAG) for the project. The users of the product are the people who want to apply to the courses. Because the product is a static webpage it’s not going to be affected by the “overcrowding” of the webpage. Because we are going to put in more pictures and videos we arI e going to make sure that the website feels fast  and functional . The limitation of the project is that it is going to be static because Chas Academy website has no backend functionality. All the functionality will be added with external links to Canvas(for the student platform) and Yrkeshögskola ( for the application process). So for if the user want to contact the school will do that externally of the platform as to login to the student platform.

  1. Relevant Facts and Assumptions - The design is going to be vivid and fluid and fast but with the increase of media material such as photo and videos is going’s to be a risk that the website is not going to be as fast and fluid as planed.
  2. The Scope of the Work —the business area or domain under study. - The scope of work is to gather all the requirements both functional and not with focus on accessibility (WCAG)
  3. Functional and Data Requirements —the things the product must do and the data manipulated by the functions - The Chas Academy Website is not going to have a back/end, so not data is going to be stored. The only data is the user data from Google analys with the scope to improve the website content quality and structure. Other functionality like add to calendar, contact form are not going to save any data.
  4. Look and Feel Requirements —the intended appearance - The main change is going to be to give the website a new makeover. Requirements like, more photo video and color in combination with a new structure(navbar footer and pages) will give the website more vivid fluid and colourful appearance.
  5. Usability and Humanity Requirements —what the product has to be if it is to be successfully used by its intended audience - The website will be used for inspiration and information purpose. The user shell find information about the courses and watch the photo and video about school activities and information about the school and staff.
  6. Performance Requirements —how fast, big, accurate, safe, reliable, robust, scalable, and long-lasting, and what capacity - The website must be fast and feel fluid so the users do not need to wait to long for the picture and video or the pages to load. Because the website has no backend it is not a big concern about security we are going to maintain to the same level as today. The WCAG basic accessibility will be guaranteed like alt tag on image, the semantic and the tab index, more info about it you will find in the backlog. For the capacity will recommend that the website should support att least 200 users together, it shouldn’t be a problem because the website has not backend.
  7. Maintainability and Support Requirements —how changeable the product must be and what support is needed - The maintenance of the website is very important. The page must be updated weekly with new content and information but the design and the structure kan remain the same with the consideration to do att list one survey a year to ask the users what they think about the website.
  8. Cultural Requirements —human and sociological factors - The website most be in Swedish as is the main language of the courses. It must have a formal language (no slang word allowed) but as the same time must be appealed to young people.
  9. Legal Requirements —conformance to applicable laws - For the official information about course plan and applying process the government rules must be follow. Only acceptable language and content on the website. No racism or discrimination content of any kind will be allowed.
  10. Open Issues —as yet unresolved issues with a possible bearing on the success of the product - Is not issue but just a further development the chatbot and the possibility that the user can interact with the website.
  11. Off-the-Shelf Solutions —ready-made components that might be used instead of building something from scratch - A lot of the structure can be reused like the footer and the navbar and content structure of pages, will just be adding new section or working the the existing one.
  12. New Problems —problems caused by the introduction of the new product - The biggest challenge will be to make the website fell faster and fluid because of the use of pictures and videos.
